About Me

I am Nathan Sachitembe, a Computer Science student specialising in Software Engineering at the University of Zambia. I am a Junior Software Engineer passionate about crafting responsive, accessible websites and scalable software solutions that make a real difference in people's lives.

My journey in software engineering has been driven by a deep curiosity about how technology can solve everyday problems. I enjoy working across the full stack — from designing clean, semantic HTML and CSS layouts to building robust back-end systems using Python, Django, and Java. I pay close attention to detail, particularly around accessibility and user experience, because I believe great software should work for everyone.

Beyond coding, I am committed to continuous learning, exploring modern web technologies, and leveraging Git and CI/CD practices to build efficient, maintainable workflows. I actively collaborate with peers on real-world projects, including a digital Point-of-Sale system aimed at helping small café restaurants transition from manual processes to efficient digital solutions.

My goal is to create software that is both functional and user-friendly, bridging the gap between technology and people — and ultimately contributing to solutions that matter at a national and global scale.
